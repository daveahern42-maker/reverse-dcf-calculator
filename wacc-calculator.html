What is WACC?

WACC is the average rate a company pays to finance its assets, weighted by the proportion of equity and debt in its capital structure.

Why it matters: WACC is your hurdle rate for DCF valuations and tells you if a company creates value. If ROIC > WACC, the company creates value. If ROIC < WACC, it destroys value.

Key Insights About WACC

  • Your discount rate for DCF: Use WACC to discount future cash flows
  • Hurdle rate for projects: New investments should return more than WACC
  • Compare to ROIC: Value creation happens when ROIC > WACC
  • Lower is better: Lower WACC means higher company valuation (all else equal)
  • Debt is cheaper than equity: Because of the tax shield (interest is tax-deductible)
  • But don't over-lever: Too much debt increases financial risk and can raise WACC
  • Industry matters: Stable businesses (utilities) have lower WACC than volatile ones (tech startups)
